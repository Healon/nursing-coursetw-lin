# Chromatic Formulary（配色處方）

## The Movement

Chromatic Formulary is the conviction that colour can be dispensed with the same measured
gravity as medicine: named, dosed, indexed, and handed over in a formulary whose authority
comes not from ornament but from the evidence of painstaking calibration. It inherits the
rationalist lineage of the great corporate modernists — the eight-bar cadence, the
unforgiving grid, the belief that a swatch is a fact — and administers it with clinical
tenderness. Every artefact of the movement must read as a laboratory document from an
imaginary discipline: the systematic study of hue as a therapeutic agent.

Space is the first instrument. Vast fields of white are not emptiness but sterile field;
margins are drawn wide the way a practitioner drapes a procedure site. Within that field,
colour arrives in precise horizontal strata — bars laid one atop another like sediment or
like the rhythm of a monitor at rest — and the composition breathes in strict intervals.
Nothing touches. Nothing crowds. Alignment is absolute, the product of countless invisible
refinements, so that a viewer senses — without being told — that a master's hand measured
every gap twice and cut once.

Colour behaves as a tonal scale, never a decoration. Each family is presented as a
graduated sequence from near-white to near-black, demonstrating its full clinical range;
the working doses — primary, shadow, accent — are then isolated and labelled like vials
drawn from the series. Blues carry trust, teals carry care, violets carry scholarship;
saturation peaks exactly once per prescription so the eye is guided, not shouted at.
Adjacent hues are tuned against each other with the patience of a colourist at the top of
the field, until the whole sheet holds together as one chord.

Typography is instrumentation, not voice. A monospaced hand records reference numbers,
tokens, and hexadecimal readings in whisper-small annotations; a serif appears rarely, as
the specimen's formal name. Text never explains — it indexes. Roman numerals, rule lines a
single hair thick, and small registration marks give the page the quiet bureaucracy of a
chart that has passed through many careful hands. If a single line of feeling is permitted,
it is set low on the page, small, like a clinician's private note.

The craft standard is non-negotiable: museum grade, meticulously crafted, laboured over as
if the sheet took weeks of ateliers and proofs. Optical corrections over mathematical ones;
rules that align to the pixel; stripes whose intervals are tuned by eye until they hum.
The finished object should feel simultaneously engineered and humane — an artefact proving
that something as ephemeral as reassurance can be studied, mapped, dosed, and prescribed.
Somewhere in the sheet, almost hidden, a single vital sign is permitted to cross the
stillness: one heartbeat in an otherwise perfect grid, the signature of the discipline the
formulary quietly serves.
